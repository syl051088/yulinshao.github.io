abstract: >
  We conduct a large-scale empirical study of covariate-adjustment strategies for
  randomized clinical trials, curating and standardizing 50 real-world RCTs from
  public repositories (29,094 participants; 574 treatment-outcome comparisons).
  We implement and evaluate 18 statistical methods combining 6 modeling approaches
  (ANCOVA, ANHECOVA, IPW, logistic g-computation, TMLE, DML) with 3
  covariate-selection strategies. Results show ANCOVA achieves 17% average variance
  reduction while ML methods provide no empirical advantage in finite samples.
  We establish evidence-based guidelines for covariate adjustment and publish all
  datasets and reproducible code on GitHub.
